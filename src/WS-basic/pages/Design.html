Design
Overview
Our design idea is to capture and denature the SARS-CoV-2 using our customized protein. We chose the receptor-binding domain (RBD) of human angiotensin-converting enzyme 2 (hACE2) as the capturer of SARS-CoV-2. The spike protein on the surface of the virus could bind with hACE2(RBD) and enter host cells via this mechanism. Moreover, we attached a protease to the bottom of the hACE2(RBD) to denature the spike proteins captured. By doing so, we could not only create an effective protective surface against SARS-CoV-2 but also deactivate the virus we captured simultaneously. The following paragraph will get into more detail about the procedures of our experiment.
Capturing
It has been proven that the receptor-binding domain of the spike protein of SARS-CoV-2 can bind to the cell receptor ACE2 when infecting human beings(see figure below). By utilizing this fact, we design our device to capture SARS-CoV-2 by hACE2 when the virus passes our device. To be more specific, we put hACE2 on gel made by us for detection, and we connect hACE2 and pepP together for inactivation.
Detection
We designed a tiny box which can detect if there is SARS-CoV-2 in the userâ€™s breathe. Inside the box, there is a piece of gel with hACE2. We add His tag on hACE2 so that His tag on hACE2 can chelate with Co2+; therefore, can be presented on the surface of our gel with Co2+. Meanwhile, we add red fluorescent protein(RFP) to hACE2 construct in order to show if one has SARS-CoV-2. Firstly, we would let the user blow in our device. After one blowing in our device, the gel with hACE2 would capture spike protein as mentioned above, if any, and then we use wash buffer to wash off hACE2 without spike. Lastly, only hACE2 with spike would be left on the gel, and RFP would show if one is infected with SARS-CoV-2 and attain detection.
Inactivating
We want to inactivate SARS-CoV-2 by preventing the virus from entering our cell. To achieve this goal, we came up with an idea which is using a protease to cut spike protein of SARS-CoV-2. Research indicated that pepP could be the appropriate protease, so we add pepP to hACE2 construct. In ideal situation, pepP would cut spike which is captured by hACE2, and thus make the virus unable to infect us.
